Ingredients:

  • 1dl aquafaba chickpea water used as egg replacement
  • 5dl flour
  • 8dl plant milkboth oat milk and soy milk works. i prefer Oatly iKaffe
  • 1tsp salt
  • Plant butter to fry in

These are my absolute favourite pancakes i've ever made (and trust me, i've tried many pancake recipes). They stay together properly and are chewy and stretchy. The recipe comes from here originally.

My suggestion is to let the butter melt completely until it stops bubbling. The first pancake is always the worst, it's a sacrifice to the pancake overlords. After this the pan will have heated up properly. You don't need much batter really, barely a dl. Make sure to tilt the pan to properly fill it. The pancakes are not supposed to be thick!

Instructions

  1. Mix flour and salt together.
  2. Add the aquafaba and 2dl of the plant milk, mix it with the flour mixture until smooth.
  3. Add 2dl plant milk at a time to prevent clumps.
  4. Let everything rest for 30 minutes before frying.
  5. Add a bunch of plant butter and get to frying on medium high heat!
